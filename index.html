My name is Shivesh Ummat, and I'm looking to improve healthcare through powerful computational methods and medical device development. I'm currently in my third year studying bioengineering with a data science focus at the University of Washington in Seattle, WA.

I'm looking for full-time, in-person summer internships at medical technology companies in roles such as data engineering and data science. Please feel free to check out some of my interests and projects below!

My Interests

Medical Devices and Physiological Signal Processing

My passion is for medical devices, especially those used by clinicians to gather relevant patient data to improve care. I am curious to learn about how devices function so I may build and improve them. It's fascinating how we can record signals from the body via devices such as pulse oximeters, EEG/EKG/EMG, and blood pressure monitors.

I've learned about the mechanisms behind translating these analog signals to readable data via signal filtering, amplifying, and conversion to digital form. I'm comfortable working with the hardware to build these processing pipelines as well as further data cleaning using methods such as digital signal processing and normalization. I would love to apply my skills working with physiological data with a team on medical device development.

Database Management and Data Engineering

I'm interested in the effective storage and accessibility of data. Data is at the heart of growth and production - informing everything from daily decisions to long-term business strategies. To harness the full power of data, we must store it in a way that is understandable, consistent, and fast.

I enjoy working with large datasets and determining optimal labeling and storage methods. I'm interested by complex time-variant or transactional data. I am able to implement data storage methods in various database management systems. I also think it's fun to write complex queries to find certain data and understand nuances in the dataset. Working on data management and design would be engaging and stimulating to my inner designer.

Clinical Informatics and Data Science

I enjoy the challenge of finding meaningful insight from highly complex datasets like multivariate clinical trials. I have a rigorous statistical knowledge that helps me distill these challenges. I love creating interactive visualizations and communicating my findings to others.

This data is meaningful to me because it has the potential to improve lives and outcomes for patients through innovative technology. I'm interested in consolidating feedback to iterate product design. I've been building my business knowledge through various startup involvement and could integrate these skills to help ensure product success. I believe I would fit well into a team that is optimizing the performance of a medical device through statistical analysis.

pHast Cam

A Low Cost Blood pH Sensor
October 2021 - Present

Project pHast Cam is working to develop a paper-based sensor that can interface with blood and be used to read pH in tandem with a smartphone app. We are creating an algorithm to read images of the sensor and analyze the color change from pH-dependent dye. Currently, I have been experimenting with different imaging environments to create reproducible lighting and image analysis.
